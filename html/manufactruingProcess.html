Our Manufacturing Process

At Yuktesh Cement Bricks, we follow a precise 8-step manufacturing process that ensures consistent quality and durability in every brick we produce.

1

Raw Material Selection

Premium quality cement, fine aggregates, and additives are carefully selected and tested for consistency and strength properties.

2

Proportioning & Mixing

Materials are precisely proportioned using automated systems and thoroughly mixed to achieve uniform consistency throughout the batch.

3

Molding & Compression

The mixture is fed into high-pressure molding machines that compress the material under controlled pressure to form dense, uniform bricks.

4

Initial Curing

Freshly molded bricks undergo initial curing in controlled humidity conditions for 24-48 hours to develop initial strength.

5

Steam Curing

Bricks are subjected to steam curing at optimal temperature and pressure conditions to accelerate the hydration process.

6

Quality Testing

Each batch undergoes rigorous testing for compressive strength, water absorption, and dimensional accuracy as per IS standards.

7

Final Curing

Bricks are air-cured for additional 14-28 days to achieve maximum strength and stability before packaging.

8

Packaging & Dispatch

Quality-approved bricks are carefully packaged, labeled, and prepared for dispatch with proper handling instructions.

Detailed Manufacturing Flow

Material Preparation & Batching

Raw Materials: Portland cement (30-35%), Fine sand (60-65%), Water (8-10%), Admixtures (1-2%)

Process: Materials are weighed using precision batching systems. Sand is screened to remove impurities and ensure proper gradation. Cement quality is verified through chemical and physical tests.

Quality Control: Each batch is tested for moisture content, particle size distribution, and chemical composition.

Mixing & Homogenization

Equipment: High-efficiency pan mixers with 3-5 minute mixing cycles

Process: Dry materials are mixed first, followed by gradual water addition. Mixing continues until uniform consistency is achieved with no lumps or dry pockets.

Quality Check: Visual inspection and consistency tests ensure proper workability and uniform color distribution.

Molding & Compaction

Equipment: Hydraulic block making machines with 15-25 MPa compaction pressure

Process: Mixed material is fed into molds and subjected to high pressure compaction. Vibration is applied simultaneously to eliminate air voids and ensure maximum density.

Output: Standard sizes - 230x110x75mm, 230x110x100mm, Custom sizes available

Curing Process

Initial Curing: 24-48 hours at ambient temperature with 80-90% humidity

Steam Curing: 8-12 hours at 60-80°C temperature and atmospheric pressure

Final Curing: 14-28 days air curing for complete strength development

Result: Achieves 80-90% of ultimate strength within 7 days

Quality Assurance

Laboratory Testing

In-house laboratory equipped with latest testing equipment for compressive strength, water absorption, and dimensional accuracy testing as per IS 2185 standards.

Process Control

Continuous monitoring of raw material quality, mixing ratios, curing conditions, and final product specifications throughout the manufacturing process.

Certification

All products are manufactured as per IS 2185:2005 standards and certified by authorized testing laboratories. Regular third-party audits ensure consistent quality.

Statistical Quality Control

Implementation of SQC methods including control charts, process capability studies, and defect prevention techniques to maintain zero-defect manufacturing.
